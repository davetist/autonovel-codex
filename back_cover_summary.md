# Back Cover Summary

When humanity’s first crewed mission to Mars disappears, the world mourns it as a tragedy. Years later, a recovery probe finds the ship crushed at a coordinate where no object exists: an invisible boundary in space, with Mars still shining beyond it, visible but unreachable.

The discovery breaks more than physics. Some people call it proof that reality is artificial. Some call it a government lie. Some begin to believe death might be a way out of a false world.

Dr. Mara Venn helped choose the dead crew. One of them was her brother.

Now Mara serves aboard *Witness*, the follow-up mission sent toward the same impossible edge. Her job is not only to understand what happened to the lost astronauts, but to decide what parts of their corrupted final recordings Earth is allowed to hear. A single leaked fragment has already driven people to violence, worship, and suicide. The truth may matter. It may also kill.

As *Witness* approaches the boundary, Mara must choose between public knowledge and private mercy, between evidence and care, and between the brother she wants to recover and the world that may not survive his final words.

## One-sentence pitch

After the first Mars mission is found crushed against an invisible edge in space, Earth begins to unravel, and Dr. Mara Venn, whose brother died aboard that mission, must decide whether releasing the astronauts’ corrupted final recordings will help humanity face the truth or push it into collapse.
