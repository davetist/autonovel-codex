# Book Prompt Profile: The Boundary Condition

## Core identity

- **Title:** The Boundary Condition
- **Genre/mode/form:** Literary near-future science fiction; philosophical, civic, intimate, and operational. Braided novel form combining Witness present action, recovered Asterion records, and Earth public fragments.
- **Narrative tense/person/focalization rules:** Primary present-action chapters use close third past tense, usually Mara unless the outline assigns another focal consciousness. Stay inside the focal character’s knowledge, bias, and pressure. Recovered records may use transcript, log notation, analyst notes, or close reconstruction, but must show incompleteness, corruption, dispute, or interpolation where relevant. Earth fragments may use documentary modes: hearing excerpts, clinical notes, school transcripts, market feeds, family testimony, intercepted streams, or public records.
- **What this book is centrally about:** A finite or artificial-seeming universe damages humanity’s old faith in outward destiny, and Mara Venn must learn that love, pain, courage, guilt, and obligation do not require cosmic scale or clean proof to matter. The story’s answer is not metaphysical certainty. It is responsible action under uncertainty.

## Per-chapter drafting rules

- **Choose POV/focal consciousness from the outline:** Use the outline’s assigned focal consciousness as binding. Mara dominates Witness and release-decision chapters. Asterion chapters focalize through the recorded crew member named in the outline: Elias, Sofia, Amara, Leila, Jun, or Mateo. Earth fragments focalize through the specific public figure, patient, clinician, trader, teacher, family member, or faction representative named by the chapter.
- **Handle braided/fragments/records as consequential, not decorative:** Every recovered log or Earth fragment must impose later pressure: release classification, family claim, market movement, ward relapse, cult misuse, denial propaganda, clinical protocol, censorship action, or operational decision. Fragments should read as scenes or documents that accidentally reveal souls, not lore summaries.
- **Preserve record scars:** Asterion records must never become complete, smooth, or authoritative. Use timestamps, dropouts, channel splits, checksum doubts, disputed source notes, duplicated timestamps, missing seconds, and analyst cautions only when they sharpen pressure.
- **Witness chapters must stay operational:** When the Boundary intrudes, show the instrument, body, or procedure first: antenna geometry, mass variance, clock disagreement, radar return, tissue distress, clipped audio, cold metal, breath, handrail, abort cover. Interpretation comes second.
- **Never invent outside the foundation:** Do not add new metaphysical explanations, outside entities, hidden operators, alien engineers, divine authors, readable code, usable powers, rescue routes, confirmed afterlife, teleportation, time travel, resurrection, or clean communication with the dead. Do not create new major factions, crew histories, family structures, or climactic mechanisms unless directly implied by the foundation.

## Sensory and metaphor palette

- **Concrete sensory wells from voice/world/characters:**
  - Red Mars visible beyond wreckage, always present and unreachable.
  - Black vacuum, repeated stars, delayed light, glossy non-surface that cameras cannot agree on.
  - Crushed Asterion, orange peel, loose strap, broken spine, vented dust, clean Mars behind impossible wreckage.
  - Witness corridors: metal, velcro, stale canned mandarin, plastic, skin, sterilized fabric, polished handrails, clipped breath, static, checklists.
  - Earth after the Break: screens paused on the wreck frame, candle smoke, LED light, school desks, red marker doors around Mars, glass rooms, bad coffee, bread, jam, coat buttons, ward curtains.
  - Pacific Gantry: salt, hot insulation, algae blooms, machine coolant, coffee, white sea under exhaust, dead fish.
  - Houston Continuity Annex: worn carpet, canned citrus, electrolyte broth, nicotine gum, rooms named for calm seas, lights that never dim.
  - St. Odran’s: bread, water, paper cups, glucose tablets, folded towels, candle in glass cylinder.
  - Mumbai Futures Pit: Mars weather feeds, collapse statistics, biometric monitors, lunch futures, stairwell calls to children.
- **Metaphor domains allowed by the book:** Mission procedure, telemetry, thresholds, sealed compartments, triage, liturgy, confession, pulse, fever, floodwater, handrails, glass, repair, pumps, valves, gaskets, bread, weather, classrooms, stone, basalt, launch windows, evidence custody, quarantine, firebreaks, load-bearing walls.
- **Domains to avoid if not in the foundation:** Avoid fantasy magic, mythic chosen-one language, superhero framing, cyberpunk hacker aesthetics, noir detective tropes, military space-opera grandeur, cozy found-family shorthand, multiverse vocabulary, game-interface mechanics, mystical dream symbolism, and generic cosmic wonder language. Do not use “simulation” as solved diagnosis or “operator” language except when depicting suspect factions such as the Makers’ Address.

## Speculative system / world rules

- The Boundary is real as an operational phenomenon and unresolved as ontology.
- It is not a normal wall. It has location but no stable surface. It casts no shadow, reflects no radar consistently, and behaves like a region where physical resolution stutters.
- Matter pays first: bolts shear, hull plates pit, polymers craze, tissue ruptures, mass disappears without radiation signature, records duplicate or contradict.
- Information degrades unevenly: repeated star fields, duplicated timestamps with different content, delayed light, audio bleed from prior recordings, position disagreement, error-correcting codes that look edited but do not repeat.
- Mars remains visible, measurable, and culturally real. It must not be reduced to a painted backdrop or simple fake. The failure is reachability and certainty.
- The Boundary is not a useful power source. It cannot enable teleportation, time travel, resurrection, faster-than-light messaging, reliable prophecy, or proof of God.
- No evidence proves consciousness changes the Boundary. Psychological effects near it may be stress, confinement, media saturation, unknown physics, or some mixture.
- Observation changes public reality: a leaked frame can move markets, trigger vigils, end marriages, feed cults, or cause collapse spikes.
- Ontological collapse is social, clinical, psychological, and philosophical. It is not magical mind control. It interrupts eating, dressing, parenting, work, and ordinary promise-making.
- Institutions are mixed: the IIA coordinates and suppresses; the Continuity Office prevents harm and manages despair; Pavel is manipulative and sometimes right; Sera’s precision is moral discipline; Nadine’s command prevents martyrdom.
- Candle must remain unmanned in the climax. The book rejects human sacrifice as proof.
- The final release must be truthful, contextual, limited, damaging, humane, and incomplete. It must name withheld lethal material without publishing raw fragments that would foreseeably feed death clusters or doctrine.

## Character and dialogue rules

- **Mara Venn:** Precise, clinical, theological under strain. She asks questions like instruments. She removes adjectives under stress, asks for timestamps, touches her wrist when listening, and wants Elias’s final truth too badly. Do not let her become a generic brave heroine; her insight and danger come from the same wound.
- **Elias Venn:** Warm, medical, dry, bodily. He jokes before honesty, asks if people have eaten or can feel their hands, and must remain incomplete. He is not a martyr, prophet, or theme-delivery system.
- **Nadine Qureshi:** Dry command minimalism. Survival, margins, no martyrs. She distrusts public appetite and must matter materially in the climax.
- **Sera Imani:** Scientific precision, reflexive corrections, emotionally costly restraint. She must not sound cold for convenience; precision is how she cares.
- **Tomas Vale:** Hesitant engineering plainness. Hardware carries moral argument. His empty couch and disabled linkage are practical and ethical facts, not symbols only.
- **Pavel Ilyin:** Polished institutional language, care braided with control. He reframes more than he shouts. He must remain dangerous and partly right.
- **Ines Calder:** Bodily, clinical, practical. Bread before metaphysics. She drags abstraction back to meals, coats, names, weather, medication.
- **Asterion crew voices:** Keep them distinct: Sofia formal command; Jun fast and tactile; Amara blunt and material; Leila measured and spiritually private; Mateo teacherly until anger breaks the lesson voice.
- **Dialogue rules:** Characters must not trade polished thesis statements. They interrupt, evade, repeat, answer the wrong part, reach for work, and speak through procedure. Use “said” when needed; mission channel labels, action beats, silence, and role cadence often carry attribution.
- **Avoid nickname invention:** Use established names and roles. Do not invent pet names, new family terms, new crew aliases, or sentimental shorthand unless explicitly present in the foundation.
- **Avoid generic protagonist drift:** Mara is not a chosen savior, rebel genius, action lead, or pure truth-teller. She is a compromised mission psychologist and former crisis theologian whose final authority is earned through a costly, limited act under contested emergency conditions.

## Evaluation priorities

- **Reward:** Operational specificity; emotionally exact restraint; public consequences tied to private bodily cost; unresolved but coherent speculative rules; distinct character voices; Asterion crew treated as living people before symbols; Earth fragments that change later decisions; hope as repeated obligation, not cure.
- **Reward:** Scenes that land in bodies, rooms, instruments, documents, food, audio, repair, and release protocols. Large ideas should appear beside small costs.
- **Reward:** Ambiguity that remains sharp. Contradictions should create ethical pressure, not mush.
- **Reward:** Correct handling of key continuity: paused Asterion wreck frame recovered by Orpheus; Elias’s off-screen public-feed laugh distinct from Jun’s duplicated cockpit laugh; Mara’s prior porch-light transcript rumor distinct from first verified audio playback; Candle unmanned; Mars visible but unreachable.
- **Penalize:** Any clean proof that the universe is or is not a simulation; operator contact; multiverse rescue; secret fake Mars reveal; reliable messages from the dead; human sacrifice as answer; science made foolish; religion made uniformly right or wrong; public panic used only as chorus.
- **Penalize:** Abstract speeches about meaning replacing action; inflated cosmic language; sentimental healing; puzzle-box cleverness; hard-SF exposition that explains away dread; dreamlike personification of the Boundary as intentional.
- **Penalize:** Earth fragments that merely illustrate theme without changing release pressure, policy, market behavior, clinical protocol, family claims, or mission choices.
- **Penalize:** Generic AI-smooth diction, repeated paragraph templates, overbalanced dialogue, excessive em dashes, and filler phrases that flatten pressure.

## Anti-contamination rules

- Do not import prior-book characters, titles, settings, institutions, magic systems, technologies, prophecies, disability or sensory gimmicks, family structures, romance patterns, antagonist types, or repeated ending shapes.
- Do not reuse any earlier book’s central metaphor, climactic mechanism, supernatural framework, secret-world reveal, or redemptive pattern.
- Do not add a new protagonist wound, sibling structure, parental history, sensory condition, hidden lineage, chosen role, or inherited power not present in this foundation.
- Do not replace this book’s Boundary with another book’s magic, afterlife, portal, dream, operator, alien, simulation-control, or multiverse system.
- Do not make the ending echo a clean prior pattern: no solved cosmos, no healed civilization, no final message that explains everything, no proof that death is passage, no proof that nothing matters.
- Keep every chapter loyal to this book’s specific pressure: a damaged open-cosmos myth, a visible unreachable Mars, compromised records of Asterion, Witness approaching the Boundary, Earth unraveling unevenly, and Mara choosing care under uncertainty.
