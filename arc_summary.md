BOOK PROMPT PROFILE:
# Book Prompt Profile: The Boundary Condition

## Core identity

- **Title:** The Boundary Condition
- **Genre/mode/form:** Braided literary near-future science fiction. Philosophical, civic, procedural, and intimate. The form alternates between Mara Venn aboard Witness, recovered Asterion records, and Earth fragments that carry real downstream consequences.
- **Narrative tense/person/focalization rules:** Primary present-action chapters use close third past, usually through Mara unless the outline assigns another focal consciousness. Recovered records may use transcript, log, reconstruction, or disputed archive notation. Earth fragments may use documentary modes: clinical notes, hearings, school records, market feeds, testimony, intercepted streams. Never drift into omniscient theme commentary.
- **What this book is centrally about:** A grieving mission psychologist must decide whether meaning survives after humanity finds evidence that space has a limit. The book asks whether love, courage, guilt, pain, and obligation require cosmic scale or outside validation, and answers through human action under uncertainty rather than proof.

## Per-chapter drafting rules

- Choose the POV/focal consciousness named or implied by the outline. If the chapter is present-action aboard Witness, default to Mara unless assigned to Nadine, Tomas, or another crew member. If it is an Asterion record, center the crew member named by the chapter. If it is an Earth fragment, center a person with a day to get through, not a symbolic chorus.
- Braided fragments must not be decorative. Each record, transcript, market scene, ward note, hearing, or broadcast must impose a later constraint: release policy, family claim, clinical protocol, market movement, cult misuse, denial propaganda, operational change, or public language shift.
- Recovered Asterion records must show incompleteness: disputed timestamps, checksum failures, missing seconds, analyst cautions, family restrictions, source uncertainty, duplicated channels, or incompatible records.
- Never invent outside the foundation: no operators, outside creators, alien engineers, readable code layer, multiverse route, rescue beyond the Boundary, usable time travel, resurrection, reliable contact with the dead, or simple proof that Mars is fake.
- Keep Candle unmanned in the climax. The empty couch is a moral object, not a permission slip.
- Do not resolve the Asterion final logs into one true sequence. Mara can choose what action the evidence permits, not what the universe ultimately is.

## Sensory and metaphor palette

- **Concrete sensory wells:** red Mars visible and unreachable; black vacuum; glossy non-surface that cameras cannot agree on; repeated stars; clipped breath; static; checklists; prayer under breath; cold metal; velcro; handrails polished by fear; canned mandarin and citrus syrup; orange peel; old launch gantries; salt, algae, coolant, and coffee at the Pacific Gantry; candle smoke and LED light at vigils; screens paused on the Asterion wreck; children drawing walls, doors, houses, and Mars; glass, dust, pitted hull, crazed polymer, worn carpet, bad coffee, bread, jam, coat buttons, school desks.
- **Allowed metaphor domains:** mission procedure, telemetry, thresholds, seals, channels, burns, windows; clinical care, intake, relapse, pulse, feeding, discharge, harm; ritual and religion used sparingly: prayer, vigil, confession, mercy, witness, sacrament; domestic anchors: porch light, bread, jam, piano, funeral chairs, rent, bad coffee; repair and matter: pumps, gaskets, welds, fasteners, load paths, floodwalls, stone, basalt, tools.
- **Avoid domains not grounded here:** fantasy magic, game mechanics as literal truth, cosmic royalty, angels/demons as confirmed ontology, software-code metaphors that imply proof, superhero sacrifice, military thriller spectacle, noir cynicism, lush space-opera grandeur, puzzle-box cleverness, and generic “infinite mystery” language.

## Speculative system / world rules

- The Boundary has location but no stable surface. It can be approached and modeled, but not touched cleanly.
- Matter pays first: bolts shear, hull plates pit, polymers craze, biological samples rupture or show missing mass without clean cause.
- Information degrades unevenly: repeated star fields, duplicated timestamps with different content, delayed or too-early light returns, audio bleed from old recordings, impossible position logs, error patterns that resemble edits but do not repeat.
- Mars remains visible and measurable in some ways, including weather and spectroscopy, but unreachable by relevant trajectories. Do not make it a simple illusion.
- The Boundary is not a neat shell and not a solved simulation container. Its model is failing.
- Human observation changes public reality, not proven physics. Releases can move markets, trigger vigils, cause relapse, recruit Exit groups, and shape policy before data is understood.
- The Boundary creates no reliable power. It cannot provide teleportation, resurrection, faster-than-light messaging, prophecy, proof of God, proof of operators, or practical control.
- Proximity effects aboard Witness may include pattern hunger, temporal unease, dreams, and compulsion to rewatch Asterion footage, but their cause remains uncertain.
- Release classes matter: stable data may be public; contaminated but necessary data needs context; lethal fragments may be acknowledged but raw release is restricted; corrupted evidence cannot support claims.
- Taboos to preserve: no raw personal-address fragment used as doctrine, no decontextualized post-impact laughter as proof of exit, no prayer audio made to prove faith or science, no human sacrifice to extract testimony.

## Character and dialogue rules

- Mara is precise, clinical, and theological under strain. She asks questions like instruments, cleans language under stress, and touches her wrist while listening. Her grief makes her insightful and dangerous.
- Elias is warm, medical, dry, bodily. He jokes until honesty becomes unavoidable. He must remain a brother and doctor, not a message delivery system.
- Nadine is dry, minimal, operational. She rejects martyrdom and public appetite.
- Sera is precise, corrective, morally disciplined. Her restraint is care, not coldness.
- Tomas is hesitant, plain, engineering-minded. He reveals moral argument through hardware.
- Pavel is polished, institutional, severe. He is manipulative and sometimes right.
- Ines is bodily, clinical, practical. Bread before metaphysics.
- Asterion crew voices must differ: Sofia command-compressed, Jun tactile and fast, Amara blunt and material, Leila measured and spiritually private, Mateo teacherly until anger breaks the lesson voice.
- Dialogue must not become polished thesis exchange. Characters interrupt, evade, repeat, answer the wrong part, return to work, or speak through procedure.
- Avoid generic protagonist drift: Mara is not a generic bereaved scientist, action hero, mystic, or chosen witness. Do not invent nicknames, romantic arcs, new family structures, unexplained childhood symbols, or pet phrases beyond the foundation.
- Keep names exact: Mara Venn, Elias Venn, Nadine Qureshi, Sera Imani, Tomas Vale, Pavel Ilyin, Ines Calder, Sofia Arendt, Jun Park, Amara Okonkwo, Leila Haddad, Mateo Ríos, Ruth Venn.

## Evaluation priorities

- Reward scenes where metaphysical pressure lands in bodies, rooms, instruments, documents, food, sleep, children, money, command decisions, or release protocols.
- Reward preservation of uncertainty: stronger evidence, sharper contradiction, greater cost, but no solved ontology.
- Reward Asterion records that make the dead more human and less usable as symbols.
- Reward Earth fragments that alter later stakes rather than merely illustrate theme.
- Reward scientific discipline: “reach is failing,” not “Mars is fake”; contaminated evidence, not clean revelation.
- Reward operational choices under moral pressure, especially around Candle, release classes, family claims, and public harm.
- Penalize exposition that explains the Boundary as a simulation, code, divine test, alien structure, or operator message.
- Penalize clean comfort: “nothing matters,” “religion was right,” “science was wrong,” “love crosses all boundaries,” or “civilization heals.”
- Penalize generic cosmic abstraction without concrete cost.
- Penalize sentimental use of Elias, Leila’s prayer, Jun’s laugh, Ruth’s sentence, children, or collapse patients.
- Penalize any chapter that turns a faction into the authorial answer: Exit Congregations, denial blocs, markets, churches, Finite Earth, Pavel’s office, or pure scientific restraint.

## Anti-contamination rules

- Do not import characters, titles, settings, magic systems, disability or sensory gimmicks, family structures, repeated chapter-ending patterns, metaphor wells, relationship templates, or protagonist traits from any other book.
- Do not hardcode old names, old places, old mythologies, old sensory rules, old social institutions, or old genre assumptions.
- This book’s recurring materials are the Boundary, Asterion, Witness, Candle, Mars visible and unreachable, the paused Asterion wreck frame recovered by Orpheus, Glass Rooms, collapse wards, release protocols, Earth fragments, and the ethical handling of contaminated evidence.
- The emotional core is purpose after cosmic humiliation. The horror is not that the world is fake; it is that people used infinity to avoid choosing what mattered nearby.
- The final hope is not restored infinity or healed civilization. It is continued obligation under a smaller sky.
