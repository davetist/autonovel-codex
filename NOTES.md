# Book Intent Notes: The Boundary Condition

David's original premise:

> The premise is how people is able to find purpose after knowing that (maybe) we live in a simulation.
>
> Finally humans try to reach Mars as the first interplanet journey but suddenly after a bit the ship's signal is lost. Another trip occurs to try to find what happened and it finds the old ship floating near where the signal was lost completely crashed, like it crashed against something, some wall.
>
> A little recognition ship goes and this time the other ship that was waiting is able to see what happens: there is a limit in space. We thought space was infinite but is not. On earth people starts speculating and there is somehow a general conspiracy/rumour/whatever that we simply live in a simulation and this simulation has limits, limits that we reached trying to travel to Mars, a planet that is shown as a far away object but is simply not reachable.
>
> How do humans find purpose after knowing everything they knew doesn't exist? How do humans find purpose after knowing that nothing else matters because nothing is real?

## North Star

This should be a book about purpose after reality loses authority.

The simulation premise must stay philosophically dangerous, not mechanically tidy. The important fracture is not “are we in a computer?” but “what happens when humanity can no longer use infinity, progress, God, history, or the stars as external validators for meaning?”

## Key Taste Constraints

- Keep the central claim uncertain: maybe simulation, maybe boundary, maybe failure of physics, maybe something worse.
- Do not solve the metaphysics too cleanly.
- Make Earth’s response feel social, religious, economic, memetic, and intimate.
- Make the Mars wall a wound in human purpose, not just a sci-fi object.
- Preserve the core question: if nothing is cosmically guaranteed, why continue?
- The answer should be earned through obligation, love, memory, and choice under uncertainty, not a slogan.

## Current Working Title

The Boundary Condition
