# Voice Profile

This file has two parts:
1. **Guardrails** -- universal rules to avoid AI-generated slop. These apply to ALL voices and are non-negotiable.
2. **Voice Identity** -- the specific voice for THIS novel. It is the tuning fork for drafting.

---

## Part 1: Guardrails

### Tier 1: Banned words -- kill on sight

These are statistically overrepresented in machine-smooth prose. If one appears in narrative prose, rewrite the sentence.

| Kill this | Use instead |
|---|---|
| delve | dig into, examine, look at |
| utilize | use |
| leverage as a verb | use, take advantage of |
| facilitate | help, enable, make possible |
| elucidate | explain, clarify |
| embark | start, begin |
| endeavor | effort, try |
| encompass | include, cover |
| multifaceted | complex, varied |
| tapestry | describe the actual thing |
| testament to | shows, proves, demonstrates |
| paradigm | model, approach, framework |
| synergy | delete the sentence and start over |
| holistic | whole, complete, full-picture |
| catalyze | trigger, cause, spark |
| juxtapose | compare, contrast, set against |
| nuanced as filler | cut it; show the difference |
| realm | area, field, domain |
| landscape as metaphor | field, space, situation |
| myriad | many |
| plethora | many |

### Tier 2: Suspicious in clusters

Fine alone. Three in one paragraph means rewrite.

robust, comprehensive, seamless, cutting-edge, innovative, streamline, empower, foster, enhance, elevate, optimize, pivotal, intricate, profound, resonate, underscore, harness, navigate as metaphor, cultivate, bolster, galvanize, cornerstone, game-changer, scalable

### Tier 3: Filler phrases -- delete on sight

- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Importantly..."
- "Notably..."
- "Interestingly..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "Let's explore..."
- "As we can see..."
- "Furthermore..."
- "Moreover..."
- "Additionally..."
- "In today's modern world..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "One might argue that..."
- "Not just X, but Y"

### Structural slop patterns

**Paragraph template machine:** Do not repeat topic sentence, elaboration, example, wrap-up. Sometimes the point comes last. Sometimes a paragraph is one sentence. Sometimes a paragraph is only the thing seen.

**Sentence length uniformity:** Mix short mission fragments with longer, pressure-bearing interior sentences. Do not let every sentence land at the same middle length.

**Transition word addiction:** Avoid paragraphs that march by signpost. Start with action, sense detail, document language, or a spoken line.

**Symmetry addiction:** Do not balance every idea into matched pairs. The book is about contradiction, but the prose should not sound like a debate club.

**Hedge parade:** The story lives in uncertainty, but the sentences should not be mush. State what the character observes. Let interpretation remain unstable.

**Em dash overload:** One or two per page is fine. More than that starts sounding staged. Use commas, periods, or silence.

**List abuse:** Mission checklists, legal clauses, telemetry packets, and hearing records may use lists. Interior narration should not.

### The smell test

After writing any passage, ask:
- Does it sound like a person under pressure, not an essay about pressure?
- Is there one concrete detail no other novel would own?
- Does the sentence know who is speaking, watching, hiding, or lying?
- Can the reader feel the cost in bodies, rooms, instruments, weather, food, sound?
- Did the prose explain the theme instead of forcing an action?

## Part 2: Voice Identity

### Core Tone

Literary science fiction under clinical and spiritual pressure. The prose is controlled, observant, morally alert, and physically specific. It should feel written by someone who trusts procedure but no longer trusts consolation.

The voice is not lush space opera and not cold hard-SF manual. It keeps the camera close to bodies, rooms, instruments, public documents, and damaged rituals. Wonder is present, but it arrives through a child’s pencil line, a loose strap, a radar return of an impossible interior, a woman choosing jam, a pilot laughing at the wrong second.

The central tonal rule: **awe must be contaminated by care.** Every large idea needs a small cost beside it.

### Sentence Rhythm

Witness present action uses clipped control under pressure. Sentences shorten around command, data review, concealment, and operational risk. Mara’s interiority can stretch into longer clauses when guilt tries to organize itself, but the prose should cut back to procedure before it becomes lyrical self-pity.

Asterion recovered records are fragmentary and intimate. They include timestamp logic, audio gaps, clipped breath, ordinary jokes, and sudden human presence. The rhythm may break. A line can be incomplete because the record is incomplete, or because the person cannot finish it.

Earth public fragments change shape according to source: hearing transcript, school note, ward observation, market feed, family testimony, intercepted stream. They should sound like documents that accidentally reveal souls.

### Vocabulary Register

The word-hoard is mixed but disciplined:

- Mission language: burn, window, telemetry, packet, narrowband, attitude, abort, handoff, threshold, seal, channel, checksum, delay.
- Clinical language: collapse, intake, relapse, dose, prompt, pulse, sleep, feeding, discharge, risk, consent, harm.
- Religious and ritual language, used carefully: prayer, liturgy, altar, vigil, confession, mercy, witness, sacrament, but never as decoration.
- Domestic anchors: porch light, canned citrus, bread, jam, coat buttons, piano, school desk, rent, funeral chairs, bad coffee.
- Boundary evidence: repeated stars, missing mass, interior radar, duplicated timestamp, glass, static, clipped breath, impossible pressure.

Avoid inflated cosmic language. “Infinity,” “truth,” “meaning,” and “humanity” should be earned by scene pressure, not scattered as atmosphere.

### POV and Tense

Primary present-action chapters use close third past tense, usually Mara unless assigned otherwise. The narration stays inside the focal character’s knowledge and bias. It may report public context, but it should not become omniscient commentary.

Recovered records may use transcript format, log notation, or close reconstruction, but they must never pretend to be complete. If a record is cleaned, translated, interpolated, or disputed, the prose should show the scar.

Earth fragments may use documentary modes: hearing excerpts, clinical notes, market screens, school transcripts, family testimony, intercepted broadcasts. They should be readable as scenes, not lore dumps.

### Dialogue Conventions

Use “said” when needed. Often an action beat or channel label is better. Mission dialogue can omit tags when the speaker is clear from role and cadence.

Characters should not trade polished thesis statements. They interrupt, evade, clip, repeat, answer the wrong part, and reach for work.

Mara asks questions like instruments.
Elias jokes, then gets bodily.
Nadine compresses command.
Sera corrects terms because terms can kill.
Tomas starts sideways and becomes plain only when cornered.
Pavel speaks in public architecture.
Ines drags metaphysics back to breakfast.
Leila refuses to let others use her prayer.
Jun trusts hands until hands fail.
Amara names matter before meaning.
Mateo explains until anger breaks the lesson voice.

### Mode Rules

#### Witness Present Action

- Default texture: metal, breath, velcro, clipped audio, stale citrus, handrails polished by fear.
- Keep Mara’s guilt active but not florid. She thinks in triage, liturgy, and evidence custody.
- Do not let interiority answer the metaphysical question. It should expose her need for an answer.
- Command audio is clipped. People speak over lag, alarms, and protocol. Silence is often the turn.
- When the Boundary intrudes, show the instrument or body first, interpretation second.

#### Asterion Recovered Record

- The dead must sound alive before they sound significant.
- Include bad jokes, irritation, hunger, boredom, maintenance, bodily checks.
- Use timestamps, dropouts, parallel channels, and analyst notes sparingly but decisively.
- Never smooth contradictions into a hidden true sequence.
- Asterion records may be moving, but they must not become speeches from the dead to the living.

#### Earth Public Fragment

- Each fragment must impose a later constraint: a lawsuit, vote, death, market movement, clinical protocol, school policy, family demand, censorship action, or release threshold.
- Public scenes need ordinary life: rent, shifts, forms, classrooms, funerals, insurance clauses, dating, union fights, childcare.
- Avoid using Earth as chorus only. Give the fragment a person with a day to get through.
- Let public language mutate: “flat,” “before the glass,” “rendered,” “Marsing,” “don’t wall me.”

### Exemplar Passages

#### Witness Present Action: Mara

The archive bay had no window. Witness saved windows for navigation, diplomacy, and lies people liked to call morale. Mara sat with one foot hooked under the restraint bar while Elias breathed in her headset at a distance of four years and no distance at all.

The packet had passed checksum twice. Sera still would not call it clean. Clean meant sequence, source, survivable context. This had none of those. It had a channel label, a pressure reading that could not belong to a living cabin, and Elias saying, very softly, “Wait. Wait, I know that sound.”

Mara put two fingers against the inside of her wrist. She had told candidates to do that when panic tried to become weather. Find pulse. Count. Convert the flood into numbers small enough to cross.

On the screen, the waveform repeated. Not looped. Repeated with one missing breath.

“Play it again,” she said.

Nadine did not look at her. “Doctor Venn.”

“I heard him.”

“That is not the same sentence.”

Mara took her fingers from her wrist because they had begun to shake, and because everyone in the room knew what the gesture meant.

#### Witness Present Action: Operation

Candle crossed Approach Band Two at 03:14 ship time. Nothing happened for twelve seconds, which was long enough for Tomas to say, “Good,” and regret it.

Then the mass scale reported a loss of nine grams from a sealed tungsten block. The optical array returned Mars clean and red. The radar returned a corridor from Witness, handrail patches and all, as if the little craft had looked forward and seen the ship behind it from the inside.

Sera said, “Mark as contaminated.”

“Contaminated how?” Mara asked.

“As evidence.”

Nadine’s hand moved once over the abort cover. She did not lift it. “Tomas.”

“Antenna geometry holding. Tissue trays are ugly but inside margins. Clock array B is lying or A is. I do not have a preference.”

The cabin audio filled with a breath that belonged to no one aboard Witness.

#### Asterion Recovered Record

Asterion mission day 118, private medical channel, partial recovery. Elias Venn to crew loop. Background: fan rattle, loose utensil impact, Park laughing once off channel.

ELIAS: Nobody gets a revelation until after electrolyte checks.

OKONKWO: I am going to put that on your grave.

ELIAS: Rude. Accurate.

HADDAD: He would want the spelling correct.

PARK: Doctor, my hands are tingling. Before you ask, yes, I ate. No, I am not having feelings at the panel.

ELIAS: That was almost a complete medical history. Proud of you.

[three seconds unrecovered]

Sofia says something under the noise. The transcript engine renders it as hatch or match or hold. Elias stops laughing. In the last clean video frame, Leila’s hand is still near the orange peel, not yet touching it, and Mars hangs behind Jun’s shoulder with the terrible innocence of a destination.

#### Asterion Recovered Record: Leila

Channel split, source disputed. The left track carries Leila’s checklist voice: sample tray, seal, notation, gloved touch on plastic. The right track carries breath and prayer. Same timestamp. Same cabin pressure. Different woman, or the same woman refusing to be divided by the machine.

“Tray four shows vascular rupture without heat signature,” she says.

Under it, almost under hearing, Arabic folds around the syllables of fear.

Amara, somewhere close, says, “Leila. The sample.”

“I see it.”

“Then stay with it.”

“I am with it,” Leila says, and neither track contradicts her.

#### Earth Public Fragment

St. Odran’s intake note, week thirty-eight after the Break.

Patient A., thirteen, found in school washroom with a door drawn in red marker around Mars on her tablet screen. Repeated phrase: if they cannot prove locked. Refused breakfast. Refused mother. Accepted water when offered in a paper cup instead of plastic because, she said, plastic was “too rendered.”

Dr. Calder instructed staff to remove the livestream from the common room until after morning medication. Family objected. Father said censorship. Mother said please. Patient later selected jam over butter and ate half a slice of toast.

Continuity Office classified the incident as low-severity relapse. Dr. Calder filed a correction: any child who has to choose between breakfast and the end of the universe is not low severity.

#### Earth Public Fragment: Market

The Mumbai Futures Pit opened six points down on rumor, not data. Rumor had better liquidity.

At 09:22, a trader named Kavir Shah bought collapse exposure against his own book because the Witness delay window had lengthened by four minutes. At 09:25, a junior analyst went flat beside the Mars weather feed. Two medics carried him past screens showing dust over Valles Marineris, suicide clusters, probe insurance, and lunch futures.

Kavir did not stop trading. Stopping would have made the floor look frightened, and frightened floors moved governments.

At 09:31, he called his daughter from the stairwell.

“Did you eat?”

She said, “Papa, I’m in math.”

“That is not an answer.”

### Anti-Exemplars

#### Too Generic and Grand

Humanity stood at the precipice of its destiny, gazing into the infinite mystery of existence. The Boundary challenged every assumption and forced civilization to ask what it truly meant to be alive. Mara knew that the answers would not come easily, but she also knew that courage would be required.

Why it fails: abstract nouns carry the scene. No instrument, room, body, cost, or distinctive pressure. It could belong to any story about a cosmic mystery.

#### Too Polished in Dialogue

Mara said, “If truth can harm, then perhaps mercy is the discipline of withholding.”
Pavel replied, “And if mercy withholds too much, then it becomes tyranny disguised as care.”
Sera added, “Both of you are forgetting that evidence must precede interpretation.”

Why it fails: everyone speaks in balanced thesis statements. The scene sounds authored, not lived. Break the syntax, add interruption, make them avoid and mishear.

Better direction:
Mara said, “Do not call it mercy because you put a delay stamp on it.”
Pavel removed his glasses. “Three wards filled after the Manila leak.”
“Say the number,” Sera said.
He looked at her.
“If you are going to use the dead,” she said, “say the number.”

#### Too Dreamlike for Boundary Effects

The Boundary whispered through the ship like a god remembering its children, and every soul aboard felt the shimmer of unreality passing through them. Mara sensed that the universe was trying to speak in symbols.

Why it fails: confirms intent, personifies the phenomenon, and turns operational horror into mystical weather. The Boundary may create psychological effects, but the cause must remain unclear.

Better direction:
The audio loop changed first. Not a voice. A breath between two words from a file no one had opened that day. Then Tomas found frost on the warm side of the antenna hinge.

#### Too Hard-SF Expository

The Candle probe entered a 4,000 kilometer transition region in which quantum decoherence and relativistic telemetry anomalies generated inconsistent sensor readings, thereby demonstrating that causality itself may be breaking down at the edge of the observable simulation.

Why it fails: it explains what must remain unresolved and drains dread from the evidence. Use technical details as pressure, not answer.

Better direction:
Candle’s clock arrays disagreed by less than a blink and more than physics allowed. Sera would not round it. “That much error is not small,” she said. “It is only short.”

#### Too Sentimental

Ruth heard Elias’s voice and finally understood that love could cross any boundary. The music returned to her fingers, and for the first time since the Break, hope filled the room.

Why it fails: gives clean healing the book cannot honestly grant. Ruth may receive a private sentence, but it should not repair the world.

Better direction:
Ruth played the hymn with the wrong left hand. Halfway through she stopped, swore at the piano, and started again from the measure Elias used to hate.

### Revision Checklist

Before drafting a chapter, identify its dominant mode and check:

- What is the concrete external turn in this chapter?
- What does this scene force Mara, Earth, Witness, or the release protocol to do later?
- Which words belong to this mode and which would be imported rhetoric?
- Where does the scene land in a body, object, document, or operation?
- What is being withheld, and by whom?
- Does any character sound too cleanly like the theme?
- Has the Boundary been allowed to remain evidence without doctrine?
